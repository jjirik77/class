What are Tokens?

In AI, a token is the basic unit of text. It's not always a whole word; sometimes it's a syllable or even a single character. Models like ChatGPT have a Context Window (a max token limit). If your prompt + the model's response exceeds this limit, the model starts to "forget" the beginning of the conversation.

"Rule of thumb: 1,000 tokens is roughly 750 words."

Method 1: Truncation

The "Quick Cut." Simply removing words from the beginning or end to fit the limit.

  • Pros: Fast, easy to automate.
  • Cons: High risk of losing critical context or instructions.

# Python Logic
def truncate(text, i):
    return ' '.join(text.split()[:-i])

# Result: Removes last i words

Method 2: Chunk Processing

Break a large document into smaller sections. Process each part separately and stitch the results together.

Caution: The AI loses "Global Context." It won't know what happened in Chunk A while reading Chunk C.

Method 3: Intelligent Summarization

Condense the core meaning. Instead of feeding a whole transcript, provide a high-level summary of the context first.

High Token Prompt:

"It's such a fine day today. The sun is out and the sky is blue. I'm wondering if you can tell me what the weather will be like tomorrow?"

Low Token (Condensed):

"Tell me the weather forecast for tomorrow."

Both achieve the same goal, but the green box saves significant "token real estate."

Method 4: Stop Word Removal

Modern LLMs are sophisticated enough to understand "Keyword-Dense" language. By removing "Stop Words" (the, is, at, which), you save space.
